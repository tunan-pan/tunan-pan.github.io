Seattle Animal Shelter Donation Redesign

Case study  Web/UX

The Problem

Seattle Animal Shelter’s current online donation experience has several friction points, including low visibility, disruptive transitions, and a complex form, which may discourage users from completing donations.

1. Navigation friction: Indirect and low-emphasis donation pathway

Rather than providing a direct entry point, the navigation routes users through a general “Get Involved” page. Here, donating is one of several options and is presented as an inline link with minimal visual hierarchy. This lack of emphasis makes it harder for high-intent users to quickly identify and access the donation flow.

Also, users visiting nonprofit sites often expect a clear and prominent donation call-to-action, especially in the top navigation bar, making this pattern inconsistent with common user expectations.

However, I decided to limit the scope of this project and only focus on optimizing the donation page and flow itself, assuming users are able to locate the page or are directed there from external sources.

2. Flow disruption: External payment system introduces friction

The donation process redirects users to an external government payment page. This abrupt transition creates a potential drop-off point, as it introduces uncertainty and can feel disconnected from the main site experience.

Additionally, key donation options (such as amount selection and fund designation) are hidden behind this link, reducing motivation for users to proceed.

3. Form complexity: Long, multi-step form increases cognitive load

The current form combines the drawbacks of both single-page and multi-step forms: each page is long and dense, while still requiring multiple steps to complete.This creates cognitive overload and uncertainty, as users may feel they have already completed significant effort, only to encounter additional steps.

Allowing users to donate to multiple funds simultaneously also introduces unnecessary decision fatigue, further increasing the likelihood of abandonment.

My Design Goals

Increase successful donation completion by:

  • Reducing friction for high-intent donors
  • Increasing trust during the payment transition
  • Simplifying and streamlining the donation process

The Constraints

  • Government website structure and design limitations
  • External payment system (.aspx)
  • Likely limited engineering resources

Researching Competitors and Best Practices

I looked at several similar organizations: Another government shelter, other well known shelters and animal organizations, and other shelters/rescues in the local Washington area.

Key Patterns / Best Practices:

  • Multi-step donation flows are commonly used and have been shown to improve completion rates (e.g. Blackbaud research) due to reduced cognitive load.
  • Additionally, it supports progressive data capture that allows the organization to re-engage users who do drop off
  • Preset donation amounts and monthly giving options reduce decision friction
  • Default selections help guide users toward common choices
  • Tangible, impact-based messaging (e.g. “$25 fills a food bowl”) helps users understand the real-world effect of their donation
  • Strong visual hierarchy and minimal first steps reduce cognitive load
  • Compelling images and strong messaging remind users about the cause they’re supporting
  • Consistent branding reinforces trust throughout the process
  • Progress indicators help users understand where they are in the flow
  • Visual cues (e.g. heart icons and subtle animations on the CARES site) can draw attention to preferred actions such as monthly donations and create moments of delight

Common Issues:

  • Single-page forms are often too long and overwhelming
  • External payment pages with inconsistent branding reduce trust
  • On mobile, key actions (such as donation amounts) are sometimes pushed below the fold, increasing friction

The User

For this project I don’t believe a full persona profile is required, but I did want to define the user and their goals:

  • User: First-time donor motivated to support animal welfare
  • Context: Lands on the donation page via search, social, or external links
  • Goal: Quickly and confidently complete a donation
  • Concerns: Trust, effort required, and clarity on how their donation will be used

Defining success

These are the metrics I would use to measure success:

  • Donation completion rate
  • Form drop-off rate
  • Time to complete donation

As this is a conceptual project, baseline metrics are unavailable. However, my informal testing showed the current flow takes approximately ___ minutes to complete.

User flow

Current flow:

  1. Land on donation page
  2. Scroll to find donation CTA (⚠️ low visibility)
  3. Redirected to external page (⚠️ potential trust drop-off)
  4. Step 1: long form (⚠️ cognitive overload, decision fatigue)
  5. Discover “Proceed to Payment Details” button at end (⚠️ unexpected additional step)
  6. Step 2: continued long form
  7. Submit & confirmation page

Proposed flow:

  1. Land on donation page with immediately visible donation options
  2. Informed redirect to secure payment page
  3. Step 1: Donation details only, with clear progress indicator throughout
  4. Step 2: Billing information
  5. Step 3: Payment details
  6. Submit & confirmation page
